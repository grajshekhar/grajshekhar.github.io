Optical Metrology and Imaging Laboratory

Welcome to the Optical Metrology and Imaging Laboratory (OMIL) at IIT Kanpur. Our goal is to develop novel optical techniques for precision metrology. The relevant application areas include surface characterization, 3D shape sensing, non-destructive inspection and biomedical imaging.

Phd and Masters positions are available. Interested students can contact me. Students with good photonics or signal processing background are strongly encouraged to apply.

Research

We work in the domain of optical coherent imaging. Here, we are mainly interested in quantitatively measuring the phase of the optical wave-field scattered from an object or specimen. We work on both the experimental and computational aspects of coherent imaging.

The experimental techniques being investigated in the lab involve:

  • Digital holography

  • Fringe Projection

  • Quantitative phase microscopy

The computational research involves the development of robust signal processing techniques in optical imaging. Some relevant problems include:

  • Phase retrieval methods

  • Phase gradient estimation

  • High performance techniques

The developed techniques and methods are tested for several practical applications in the areas of Non-destructive Precision Metrology, Nanoscale Topography, Shape Detection, and Biomedical Imaging.
